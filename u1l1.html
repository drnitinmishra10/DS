1. Introduction to Data Science

Definition:

Data Science is an interdisciplinary field that combines statistics, mathematics, programming, machine learning, and domain knowledge to collect, process, analyze, and interpret data in order to extract meaningful insights and support intelligent decision-making.

In today's digital world, enormous amounts of data are generated every second through smartphones, social media, online shopping, healthcare systems, banks, educational institutions, and IoT devices. Data Science helps organizations convert this raw data into valuable knowledge.

2. Why Do We Need Data Science?

  • To make better business decisions.
  • To predict future trends.
  • To improve customer satisfaction.
  • To automate repetitive tasks.
  • To detect fraud and cyber attacks.
  • To improve healthcare diagnosis.
  • To recommend products and services.
  • To analyze large volumes of data efficiently.
Example:

Amazon recommends products based on previous purchases. Netflix recommends movies according to viewing history. Google Maps suggests the fastest route using traffic data. Banks detect fraudulent transactions using Data Science techniques.

3. What is Data?

Data refers to raw facts, numbers, observations, symbols, or measurements that have not yet been processed.
Example Data
Student Marks 75, 82, 90, 68
Temperature 32°C, 35°C
Bank Balance ₹50,000

4. What is Information?

Information is processed and organized data that provides meaning and context.

Example: "Student Rahul scored 90 marks and secured First Division." Here, raw marks have been processed to provide useful information.

5. Data vs Information

Data Information
Raw facts Processed facts
No context Meaningful
Input Output
Unorganized Organized

6. Knowledge and Wisdom

Level Description
Data Raw facts
Information Meaningful data
Knowledge Experience gained from information
Wisdom Ability to make correct decisions
Remember: Data → Information → Knowledge → Wisdom This is known as the DIKW Pyramid.

  4. Analyze Data

10. Characteristics of Data Science

  • Data-driven
  • Interdisciplinary field
  • Predictive and descriptive analysis
  • Decision support system
  • Uses Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning
  • Handles structured and unstructured data

11. Advantages of Data Science

  • Improves decision making
  • Enhances productivity
  • Reduces operational cost
  • Predicts customer behavior
  • Automates business processes
  • Supports innovation

12. Limitations

  • Requires quality data.
  • Privacy and security concerns.
  • Requires skilled professionals.
  • High computational cost.
  • Data bias may affect predictions.

13. Summary

  • Data Science converts raw data into meaningful insights.
  • It integrates statistics, programming, machine learning, and domain knowledge.
  • Organizations use Data Science to improve decision making.
  • Applications exist in healthcare, finance, education, agriculture, and many other domains.
